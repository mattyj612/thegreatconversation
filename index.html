The Great Conversation

Within each of us there is a drive to do extraordinary things and move the human project forward.

We are told we should maximize our agency and “just do things.” But how do we know which things are worth doing?

This question cannot be answered by moving fast. It requires us to meet our ancestors—the thinkers, tinkerers, and misfits whose names we might never know, but whose work made ours possible. We are all part of something bigger than ourselves; inheritors of a grand tradition.

Technology is the process of turning ideas into tools. Ideas are not simply born, they emerge from dialogue about what it means to be human.

You have been part of this conversation your entire life. You just haven't been formally introduced.

On May 27th, we invite you to step into this tradition.

The Toronto Society, supported by Notion, Wealthsimple, Ada, Ambrook, and GPTZero, presents The Great Conversation

The history of technology has been told inside-out. Our tools—steam engines, silicon chips—take center stage, but the true innovation is within ourselves: the technologists who discover, enact, and transmit knowledge between generations. And technologists do not merely build; we use the things we build to shape the world around us, through politics, media, medicine, culture, finance, and beyond.

We focus on the tools because that's what we can most readily see. But knowledge lies in the builder, not the building.
